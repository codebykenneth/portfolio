My Story as an Frontend Developer

From first line of code to where I am today

I didn't start out as a developer. I was just someone who loved solving puzzles and making things look good. My journey began when I stumbled upon my first line of HTML while trying to customize a profile page. That small spark turned into late nights learning CSS, countless YouTube tutorials, and the magical moment when I wrote my first JavaScript function that actually worked.

"The best code is invisible—it just works, beautifully."

Over the years, I've grown from copying code snippets to building complete web applications from scratch. I've worked with startups burning with fresh ideas and agencies crafting digital experiences for brands worldwide. Every project taught me something new — whether it was mastering a new framework, optimizing performance, or understanding what users truly need.

What drives me? It's that moment when design meets functionality. When a button doesn't just click, but feels satisfying. When a page doesn't just load, but flows beautifully. I believe the best technology is the kind you don't notice because it simply works.
